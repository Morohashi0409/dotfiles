# note Editorial Signals

This file summarizes the signals used by `note-impact-checker`.

## Research Notes

### sumini
Source:
- https://note.com/sumini/n/ne85df1da8866

Relevant for scoring:
- image / caption / embed handling cannot be treated as trivial markdown
- note-specific publishing friction is real
- articles that rely on markdown rendering assumptions lose polish at publish time

### マナミ
Source:
- https://note.com/mana2518/n/nc891ff5c720f

Relevant for scoring:
- title should communicate the value
- conclusion-first titles are often stronger than mystery-first titles
- concrete words, numbers, and situations improve clickability
- important keywords should come early
- longer titles can still work if they are clearer
- overpromising is harmful

### フクロウ
Source:
- https://note.com/smart_logic/n/n06d0acf9ffb2

Relevant for scoring:
- readers judge quickly on mobile
- title + first 2-3 lines carry entry performance
- note success is designed through structure, line breaks, image placement, and endings
- the article is often the first touchpoint before the profile

## What Strong note Articles Usually Do

- one article, one theme
- a title that already contains the reward
- opening that makes the reader feel “this is about me”
- concrete examples over abstractions
- mobile-friendly rhythm
- images placed where understanding or pacing improves
- closing that opens the next step naturally

## What Weak note Articles Often Do

- broad vague titles
- multiple themes fighting each other
- slow openings
- dense paragraphs
- decorative images with no editorial purpose
- ad-like CTAs
- generic statements that could fit any topic

## RASHISA-Specific Interpretation

When scoring RASHISA notes, emphasize:

- single-type or single-question framing
- hidden-strength and self/other-gap insights
- practical value for self-analysis, relationships, or work
- honest framing around what the article can and cannot claim
